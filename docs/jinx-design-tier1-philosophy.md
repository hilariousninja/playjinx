# JINX — Design Philosophy (Tier 1)

A short reference covering mission, principles, visual identity, and the handful of decisions that define the product. ~10 pages.

---

## 1. Mission

JINX is a **daily crowd prediction game**. Three two-word prompts a day (e.g. MISTAKE + RIVER). Players write the first single word that bridges them. You don't win by being clever — you win by matching the crowd, and especially your friends.

**North star:** *"Did you say the same thing as your mates?"*

If a feature serves that sentence, it stays. If it only serves "did you score well?", it gets cut.

---

## 2. Design principles (in priority order)

1. **Crowd prediction, not puzzle-solving.** The fun is matching humans, not finding a "right" answer. Never show a correct answer, never gamify with hint systems.
2. **Social payoff over stats.** The first thing after submitting should be a friend comparison, not a personal scorecard. Streaks, accuracy %, leaderboards are deliberately absent.
3. **Honesty over hype.** Group activity bars reflect real aggregate behaviour. Empty states say "no jinxes yet today" — never fake excitement.
4. **Mobile-first, thumb-first.** Designed for one-handed play in 60 seconds. Desktop is a courtesy, not a target.

---

## 3. Visual identity (V8)

- **Primary:** Amber `#D97706` — reserved for jinxes (matches) and primary CTAs.
- **Background:** Cream `#F6F4EF` — warm, low-glare, distinctly not "tech white".
- **Type:** Space Grotesk throughout. 52px header on every screen for instant recognition.
- **No dark mode for players.** Cream is the brand.
- **Creator dashboard** is the exception: light off-white with blue accents, separate from player palette.

---

## 4. Information architecture

Three modes, persistent bottom nav:

- **Play** — today's three prompts, results, archive of past days.
- **Groups** — recurring social groups + one-off challenge rooms.
- **Archive** — unified day-by-day history feed.

Creator dashboard lives at `/dashboard`, gated by Supabase email/password auth.

---

## 5. Defining decisions

- **One answer per day, reused everywhere.** Your submission for today appears in every challenge link and every group you're in. No re-answering for different audiences.
- **Exactly three prompts a day**, generated by `pg_cron`, locked once anyone plays.
- **Answer normalization** (case, plurals, typos, and word forms like drive/driving/drove or happy/happiness) is automatic — players never feel punished for spelling or wording. Their own card always shows exactly what they typed.
- **Persistent challenge links** (`/c/:token`) stay valid forever for that day's set.
- **Recurring groups** (`/g/:slug`) build a shared memory of who jinxes whom.

---

## 6. What we rejected (and why)

| Idea | Why rejected |
|------|--------------|
| Dark mode for players | Cream is the brand identity |
| Streaks / accuracy % | Reframes the game as personal performance |
| Anonymous signups | Identity matters for social memory |
| Auto-confirm email | Standard verification flow only |
| Per-user stats dashboard | Contradicts "social payoff over stats" |
| Weekly recap | Considered, gated until enough data exists |
